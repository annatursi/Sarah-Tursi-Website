College Planning

The key to overcoming family conflict regarding college, lack of academic performance, motivation, grades, and pending adulthood is by having a third party mediate and guide the situation. Sarah Tursi can help your teen reach their full potential.

Each year of high school has its own chronology and it is important to establish a roadmap for how to navigate both current high school academic needs and anticipation of college. Academically your child needs to be nurtured to find their strengths, self-motivation, and good fit. Schools and outside prep centers cannot offer guidance in balancing your child's academic self, relational self, and emotional self. The four years of high school are short, but the lasting impact of the decisions made can remain for a lifetime.

Freshman Year

This year is all about choices, joining, and deciding who your child will be for the next four years. Your child needs to set and achieve academic, relational, and school-wide goals in order to have a smooth transition to high school and to set the stage for future decisions. Sarah Tursi will establish a clear path for your family's next four years.

Sophomore Year

Your child has an established high school transcript with grades that directly impact college acceptance. Additionally, you're anticipating the transition to having a student driver and possibly a child who would be working for the first time. With new found freedom and independence, comes anxiety and tension within families. Sarah Tursi will guide conversations regarding motivation, independence, trust, and appropriate limit setting.

Junior Year

Junior year is unquestionably the most difficult year of high school. The academic rigor, increased demand for participation in extra-curricular activies, academic testing, sleep deprevation, and increased social life create anxiety, uncertainty, and at times depressive feelings in even the highest functioning adolescents. Sarah Tursi will provide nurturing support and clear direction through this busiest year.

Senior Year

Starting in the summer before senior year, the pressure of college applications, college selection, and transition from home weighs heavy on every member of your family. Sarah Tursi will help your child to gather the insight to write strong, introspective essays and select colleges that are truly a good fit for their whole person and that will create a lifetime of success.

College Support

The transition to college can be fraught with anxiety, uncertainty, and sadness about leaving dear friends and family. This can be further complicated by feeling that college is not as spectacular as anticipated. Many students report feelings of depression and anxiety and some need to return home. Sarah Tursi can engage students away at school and during school vacations to help them better evaluate their emotional needs during this transitional time.

Transition to Adulthood

For some, 18 marks the onset of adult responsibility and for many, this onset is delayed until college graduation and sometimes long after. Every new adult faces challenges in setting their foundation for their independent life. Issues with financial independence, relationships, and emancipation can create anxiety and tension that can be resolved with the support of a therapist. Sarah Tursi will reason through life goals and transitions to help establish a strong adult foundation.
